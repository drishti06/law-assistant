INDIAN LABOUR LAWS - KEY PROVISIONS

India has consolidated 29 central labour laws into 4 Labour Codes (enacted but notification pending in several states):
1. Code on Wages, 2019
2. Industrial Relations Code, 2020
3. Code on Social Security, 2020
4. Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code, 2020

EXISTING KEY LAWS (still applicable until Labour Codes are fully notified):

PAYMENT OF WAGES ACT, 1936:
- Applies to persons employed in factory, railway, or industrial establishments earning up to Rs. 24,000 per month
- Wages must be paid before 7th of each month (for establishments with less than 1000 workers) or before 10th (for more than 1000 workers)
- Authorized deductions: fines, absence from duty, damage/loss, housing accommodation, income tax, court orders, PF contributions, ESI, cooperative society dues, insurance premium, advance payment

MINIMUM WAGES ACT, 1948:
- The Central and State Governments fix minimum wages for scheduled employments
- Wages vary by state and type of employment
- Payment below minimum wages is an offence
- Complaint can be filed before the authority appointed under the Act

EMPLOYEES' PROVIDENT FUND AND MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS ACT, 1952 (EPF):
- Applicable to establishments with 20 or more employees
- Employee contribution: 12% of basic wages + DA
- Employer contribution: 12% of basic wages + DA (3.67% to EPF, 8.33% to EPS)
- Employee can contribute more (Voluntary Provident Fund)
- EPF balance can be withdrawn on retirement (58 years), resignation (after 2 months of unemployment), for housing, medical emergency, education, marriage
- Nomination is important; in absence of nomination, family members are entitled

EMPLOYEES' STATE INSURANCE ACT, 1948 (ESI):
- Applicable to factories/establishments with 10 or more employees
- Applies to employees earning up to Rs. 21,000 per month
- Employee contribution: 0.75% of wages
- Employer contribution: 3.25% of wages
- Benefits: Medical benefit, sickness benefit, maternity benefit, disablement benefit, dependants' benefit, funeral expenses

PAYMENT OF GRATUITY ACT, 1972:
- Applicable to establishments with 10 or more employees
- Employee must complete 5 years of continuous service (4 years 240 days considered as 5 years in certain cases)
- Formula: Last drawn salary x 15/26 x number of years of service
- Maximum gratuity: Rs. 20 lakhs (w.e.f. March 29, 2018)
- Gratuity is payable on superannuation, retirement, resignation, death, or disablement
- Forfeiture: Only if termination is for willful omission or negligence causing damage, or for riotous or disorderly conduct or any act of violence, or for offence involving moral turpitude

MATERNITY BENEFIT ACT, 1961 (as amended in 2017):
- Applicable to establishments with 10 or more employees
- Maternity leave: 26 weeks for first two surviving children (8 weeks pre-delivery, 18 weeks post-delivery)
- 12 weeks for third child onwards
- Adoptive and commissioning mothers: 12 weeks from date of adoption/handover
- Work from home option may be available after 26 weeks
- Creche facility mandatory for establishments with 50 or more employees
- No termination during maternity leave

SEXUAL HARASSMENT OF WOMEN AT WORKPLACE (PREVENTION, PROHIBITION AND REDRESSAL) ACT, 2013 (POSH ACT):
- Every employer must constitute an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) at each office/branch with 10 or more employees
- Applies to all women, irrespective of age or employment status (regular, temporary, ad hoc, daily wage)
- Sexual harassment includes: physical contact and advances, demand for sexual favours, sexually coloured remarks, showing pornography, any unwelcome sexual gesture
- Complaint must be made within 3 months of the last incident (extendable by 3 months)
- ICC must complete inquiry within 90 days
- Penalties for non-compliance by employer: fine up to Rs. 50,000 (first offence), cancellation of license (repeated offence)

INDUSTRIAL DISPUTES ACT, 1947:
- Defines procedures for investigation and settlement of industrial disputes
- Retrenchment: Employer must give one month notice or pay in lieu thereof, and compensation of 15 days' average pay for every completed year of service
- Prior permission of appropriate Government required for lay-off, retrenchment, or closure in establishments with 100 or more workers
- Workman who has been in continuous service for not less than one year cannot be retrenched without following proper procedure

SHOPS AND ESTABLISHMENTS ACT:
- State-specific legislation regulating conditions of work in shops and commercial establishments
- Covers working hours, rest intervals, overtime, holidays, leave, employment of women and children, termination of service
- Registration is mandatory for all shops and commercial establishments
